About Gem County Fire & EMS
Our Mission

Providing emrgency fire suppression and prevention activities to Fire District #1 residents and rescue-EMS response to all of Gem County and our mutual aid partners.

Our Story

Gem County Fire District #1 was formed in 1953 under Idaho Code, Title 31, Chapter 14, Fire Protection District. The original boundaries were approximately Black Canyon Dam to the east, Black Canyon Irrigation Canal south and west to the Payette County Line as a south boundary, Farmers Irrigation Canal from the dam, west to the Payette County Line as th e north boundary and the Payette, Gem County line as the west boundary. this original area was approximately 78 square miles. Since 2007 the district has grown to almost three times its original size through annexation. The fire district now protects all of the area above Freezeout Hill including most of the area adjacent to the Town of Pearl area and the west slope of Squaw Butte for a total of 10,000 residents in 250 square miles.

General Information

The Fire District maintains two (3) fire stations; one in Emmett, built in 1983 and one in Letha, built in 2002, and one in the Squaw Butte area. The stations are manned by38 trained volunteer firefighters who are from various backgrounds from farmers to executives. These firefighters are paid per call then hourly The Fire Distict has 5 engines, 3 water tenders, 1 ladder truck, 3 brush trucks, 1 rescue squad, 1 station vehicle and 1 commad vehicle
